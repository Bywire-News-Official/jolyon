About

Hello! I’m Jolyon, thanks for visiting my website. I do a lot of things in and around the world of entertainment and comedy because why not use every possible avenue to make fun of this totally bat shit crazy insane world we are living in today. From writing & performing, to podcasting to creating entertainment formats all my work seeks to subvert expectation and make you laugh your little cotton socks off. Most of the time with a satirical undertone. Because I’m nice like that.

I’ve been on BAFTA award winning telly shows that I’ve written and created with my mate from school Heydon Prowse such as BBC3’s THE REVOLUTION WILL BE TELEVISED, BREXAGEDDON & BBC2’s REVOLTING where I've played a variety of crazy characters. I’ve also been on AN IDIOTS GUIDE TO POLITICS & C4’s MINISTRY OF JUSTICE being little old me. So I guess I can do both!

WRITING & PERFORMING TV

In 2013 we won a BAFTA for our BBC3 programme The Revolution Will Be Televised, which I developed with Joe Wade and Heydon Prowse. The Guardian described it as “Sacha Baron Cohen with a bit more substance” and the show went on to record 3 successful series. BBC3 then went on to commission An Idiot’s Guide to Politics which I stared in. In in 2015 the BBC commissioned the Revolution spin off Revolution Presents: Democracy Dealers. Next in my BBC career, came the BBC2 series Revolting – which I wrote and starred in as well (bit of a pattern developing here don’t you think).

Revolting, also produced by Hat Trick Productions, aired on BBC2 in January 2017. It is estimated to have reached 75M Facebook profiles with its lead sketch The Real Housewives Of ISIS hitting 30M views and becoming a global news story. The sketch was the 8th most shared in BBC history. That’s a lot of eyeballs.

The end of 2018 we released a three part series Ministry of Justice on Channel 4, which wrote and performed In the fast paced, anarchic three-part series we go undercover to explore drugs, corrupt big businesses and the UK’s international weapons trade in order to expose crime, injustice and anti-social behavior in their own provocative comedic style. You know really easy stuff to film as always!

PODCASTING

Alongside Heydon Prowse, I created the NEWS ROAST podcast. It hit the UK top ten early in 2017 with well over 1M downloads and featured guests like Adam Buxton, Lily Allen and Romesh Ranganathan joining them both for three courses of compelling conversation. We had a great laugh doing it and they are just as relevant today as they were when we recorded them.

In 2017 I joined forces with Whistledown Productions to produce Spotify’s first ever Spotify Original satirical podcast “We Need To Talk About”, which I created and hosted. The show, which deep dived into the subjects capturing the news agenda with a mixture of irreverence chat and satirical humour, became one of Spotify’s most successful Originals globally. Recorded in LA & London “We Need To Talk About” featured Hollywood Stars like Will Arnett & Jason Isaacs, comedians Russell Kane, Asim Chaudhry and Kiri Pritchard McLean and a host of MP’s and political commentators.

Doing a weekly returning satirical show taught me a huge amount. We had awesome writers and such great guests and one day I’d love to have a crack at doing a similar satirical weekly magazine show on Telly.

PRODUCING & FORMAT CREATION

I’m the Associate Producer and Creator of 2018’s ITV2 hip-hop-themed panel show Dont Hate The Playaz. The show was described by Digital spy as “instantly everyone’s new favourite TV show”. Hosted by Jordan Stephens and featuring Maya Jama – it was certainly something fresh, new and exciting for the channel. In 2019 the show was nominated for best entertainment show at The Royal Television Society (RTS) Awards and went on to record a second series that summer. The Second series, filmed at Elstree Studios featured comedians like Katherin Tyan and Micheal Dapper and rappers like Myst and Aitch.

Making these kind of bold stand out entertainment formats has been a lot of fun and I can see myself developing more in there future.
